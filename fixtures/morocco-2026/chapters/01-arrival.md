# Arrival

The taxi from the airport takes the long way, because the medina is closed
to cars and the driver wants to drop us as close to the gate as he can.
Past the walls the city changes — the wide boulevards narrow into lanes
the width of a donkey cart, and then into something smaller than that.

![Looking down into a quiet alley off the main square](./assets/arrival/alley.jpg)

We find the riad after twenty minutes of wrong turns. The door is small.
Inside is a courtyard with a tiled fountain, three lemon trees, and a cat
asleep on a low wall.

:::gallery{layout=tile gap=12}
![The courtyard at dusk](./assets/arrival/courtyard.jpg)
![Tea on the low table](./assets/arrival/tea.jpg "First mint tea")
![Lemons on the tree](./assets/arrival/lemons.jpg)
:::

By the time we get to the roof the call to prayer is starting, and it
seems to come from every direction at once. Tomorrow we will go down into
[[02-the-market|the market at Jemaa el-Fnaa]] before sunrise, but tonight
the city is just rooflines and antennae and the sound.

:::quote-card{author="Paul Bowles" source="The Sheltering Sky" year=1949 variant=pulled}
The sky here is no longer a *known* thing.
:::
