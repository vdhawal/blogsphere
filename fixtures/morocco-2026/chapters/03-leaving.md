# Leaving for the Atlas

The car is small and the road is the N9, and within an hour of leaving
the city behind we are climbing. The Tizi n'Tichka pass is one switchback
after another, with the same scrap of valley appearing below the road from
slightly different angles for what feels like a very long time.

:::gallery{layout=fullbleed}
![The road climbing into the High Atlas](./assets/leaving/road.jpg)
:::

I keep thinking about [[02-the-market#the-market-at-dawn|the square at
dawn]] — how completely it had emptied itself by the time we walked back
through it at eleven, as if the morning had happened in another country.

::map{center="31.3000,-7.4000" zoom=9 markers="31.6295,-7.9811:Marrakech|31.2786,-7.3722:Tizi n'Tichka|30.9189,-6.8939:Ouarzazate" style=outdoors height=480}

There is a viewpoint at the top of the pass with a small stall that sells
oranges and almonds. We stop. The air is colder than it has any right to
be and you can see, very faintly, snow on the further peaks.

:::quote-card{author="Edith Wharton" source="In Morocco" year=1920 variant=framed}
The whole scene was so unreal that one had the feeling of having strayed by accident across the threshold of a dream.
:::
