# The Market at Dawn

We leave the riad at five. The lanes are empty enough that you can hear
your own footsteps echo, which is something I did not expect to be true
of any part of Marrakech, ever. By the time we reach Jemaa el-Fnaa the
stalls are mostly still skeletons — wooden frames, folded tarpaulins,
men in djellabas drinking tea and waiting for the day.

The tea sellers are first — small carts, tall silver pots, glasses
stacked in towers. One of them lets us photograph him pouring from a
ridiculous height into a glass on the ground, never spilling, while his
friend watches and laughs.

::video[A vendor unpacks dates and figs as the square wakes up.]{src=./assets/market/dates.mp4 poster=./assets/market/dates-poster.jpg muted=true controls=true}

By seven the square has turned over completely. The night-market frames
are gone, the day-stalls are up, and what was [[01-arrival|the quiet city]]
from the rooftop yesterday is now full of voices, motorbikes, and the
smell of bread.

:::quote-card{author="Elias Canetti" source="The Voices of Marrakesh" year=1968}
There were cries that I shall never forget. They came up from the depth, and they belonged to no one.
:::

Tomorrow we go south. [[03-leaving]].
